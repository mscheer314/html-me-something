Montana Trip

In July 2019, my family and I got to take a trip to visit friends that live in Whitefish, Montana. This was the first roadtrip that we had made together as a family. There were 6 of us.... In a minivan for 4000 miles.... We all survived.

Omaha Stormchaser Game

Our first stop was Omaha, Nebraska. I have a thing for visiting minor league baseball games whenever I travel. I love baseball and I especially enjoy the relaxed atmosphere of minor league games. The smaller scale of the crowd and stadium allows for a great experience watching the players closely. As a family activity, minor league baseball teams make a lot of effort to make the experience engaging for the whole family. There are often games or activities for kids to do if they are not focused on the game itself. We had a blast.

Ingalls Homestead - De Smet South Dakota

My wife and daughters had recently finished reading through the Little House on the Praire books. We planned to spend the night at one of the places in the books, since it was on our way to Montana. We spend two evenings at the Ingalls Homestead. We enjoyed making crafts and slowing down while we appreciated the praire.

Badlands National Park - South Dakota

This was one of my favorite stops on the trip. The landscape was unlike anything I had ever seen before. Apparently, this is caused by erosion. It was striking.

St Ignatius Mission - Flathead Reservation, Montana

Next, we visited a church in Flathead Reservation dedicated to our youngest child's namesake - Ignatius of Loyola. This is a National Historic Site, built in 1891. We enjoyed the artwork and the atmosphere.

Glacier National Park - Montana

Our time in Glacier National Park was wonderful. We hiked for miles within the park and it seemed that every location that we went to us more stunning and beautiful than the last.

Going to the Sun Road, Glacier National Park - Montana

Within Glacier National Park, there is a road called the Going to the Sun Road which basically goes up a mountain with a very steep drop immediately at the edge of the road. The road is narrow enough that two cars would have a very difficult time passing each other. It made for a good deal of nervous energy but the view of absolutely amazing.

Rodeo - Columbia Falls, Montana

Before leaving to come back to Missouri, we visited a local rodeo with our friends. My kids were so interested to see children as young as them riding horses, chasing down calves, and surviving being thrown from bucking horses.
